What is a Hatchback?

Definition

A hatchback is a car with a rear door that swings upward, providing access to the vehicle's cargo area. They are compact, fuel-efficient, and versatile, making them popular for urban and suburban driving.

Who is it Suitable For?

Hatchbacks are perfect for city dwellers, small families, and anyone looking for a practical, compact vehicle for everyday use.

Advantages

  • Compact and easy to maneuver
  • Great fuel efficiency
  • Practical for daily commutes
  • Ample rear storage space

Disadvantages

  • Limited rear seat space
  • Less powerful engines compared to sedans
  • Not ideal for long road trips
